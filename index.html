Education

Università degli studi di Torino
Universitat Politècnica de València

PhD. in Computer Science
The Ph.D. thesis in Computer Science, titled “Sarcasm and Implicitness in Abusive Language Detection: A Multilingual Perspective”, examines the implicit manifestations of abusive language, looking especially at the role of humour. Starting from the linguistic and computational analysis of the characteristics of the implicit abusive language against women, immigrants and cultural minorities in a multilingual scenario, we formulated the hypothesis that information about the presence of sarcasm could help to improve the detection of hateful messages, especially when they are camouflaged as sarcastic. Observing that sarcasm is a recurrent element in this kind of texts, and tends to affect the accuracy of the systems of recognition, we focused on the understanding of the role of ironic language in hateful messages. We examined the multilingual characteristics of irony and especially of sarcasm, validating, with experiments of classification, these traits as features. Finally, corroborated the importance of its role, we evaluated, looking also at the significance of the results, the benefits of ironic awareness in systems of hate speech detection.
Grade: Excellent Cum Laude
This thesis, developed in co-tutelle between Università degli Studi Di Torino and Universitat Politècnica de València, has obtained International Doctorate mention.

Alma Mater Studiorum – Università di Bologna

Master degree in Italian Studies, European Literary Cultures, Linguistics
The thesis describes the development of a rule-based system of irony detection in Italian tweets, exploiting different engineered linguistic features proposed by linguistic and rethorical literature about irony. This system was designed and tested on French and Italian data during the ERASMUS studies at the University of Jean Jaurès (Toulouse, France), and with it, I participated in the SENTIPOLC competition at EVALITA 2016 (see Publications) reaching the third place in the ranking.
Grade: 110/110
September 2013 - July 2016

Università degli studi di Palermo

Bachelor degree in Communication for Media and Institutions
The thesis titled "Multi-word expressions: problems of synonymy and of automatic translation" examines how some Italian words are commonly used, and normally classified as nouns, actually are parts of particular locutions. The problem emerges in the automatic translation of these expressions, because the translators do not "interpret" the locution enterely. We tested different Machine Translation systems, noting how these words are interpreted as independent of the other terms, altering its whole meaning of the multi-word expressions.
Grade: 110/110 Cum Laude
September 2009 - February 2013
